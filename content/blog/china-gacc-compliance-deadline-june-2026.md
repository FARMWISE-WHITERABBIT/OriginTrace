## The Deadline Is Real — And It Is Close

On 1 June 2026, China's General Administration of Customs (GACC) will begin enforcing mandatory overseas facility registration requirements for all food importers shipping into the Chinese market. Facilities that are not registered in the GACC overseas facility registry by that date will face shipment refusal at Chinese ports of entry, with no grace period.

This is not a procedural update. It is a hard enforcement trigger. Shipments from unregistered facilities will be turned away regardless of the commodity's compliance with other import requirements such as phytosanitary certificates, fumigation declarations, or quality inspection results.

If you export agricultural commodities — cocoa, cashew, ginger, sesame, palm oil, or any processed food ingredient — to buyers in China, this deadline applies to you.

---

## What GACC Registration Requires

The GACC Overseas Manufacturer Registration system, governed by Order No. 248 (General Administration of Customs Decree No. 248, 2021) and its subsequent implementation notices, requires the following from every overseas food facility:

**Basic facility registration** — your processing or handling facility must be registered in the GACC system with accurate details including facility name, address, production scope, and relevant certifications. Registration is submitted electronically through the GACC official portal or through the competent authority in your country of export.

**Food safety management system documentation** — registering facilities must demonstrate a functioning food safety management system. In practice this means HACCP certification or equivalent, pest control records, sanitation procedures, and traceability documentation showing how your products can be traced back to origin.

**Traceability to source** — GACC specifically requires that imported food can be traced back to the original production source. For agricultural commodities this means farm-level origin data: who grew it, where, and what inputs were applied. A warehouse address is not sufficient.

**Labelling compliance** — all products destined for China must comply with GB 7718 (General Standard for the Labelling of Prepackaged Foods) and GB 28050 (Nutrition Labelling Standard). Labels must be in Mandarin Chinese and include the GACC registration number.

**Country-specific veterinary or phytosanitary protocols** — many commodities are subject to bilateral protocols between China and the country of export. Your competent national authority (in Nigeria, this is NAQS for agricultural products) must be the submitting body for your registration.

## Why Nigerian Agricultural Exporters Are Particularly Exposed

Nigeria is one of the largest exporters of agricultural commodities to China, with ginger, sesame, and cashew being among the most significant volume commodities. The challenge for Nigerian exporters is structural: most supply chain documentation stops at the aggregator or warehouse level. Farm-level traceability — the GPS coordinates, farmer identification, and input records that GACC's traceability requirements imply — has historically not been collected systematically.

GACC registration can be completed and approved, but if your first shipment post-June 2026 is inspected by Chinese customs and the traceability data on your documentation cannot be verified back to farm level, you face two risks: shipment detention for additional inspection, and potential removal from the GACC registry.

The registration gets you through the gate. The traceability data keeps you through the gate on an ongoing basis.

---

## The Three-Step Action Plan for Exporters

**Step 1 — Register your facility immediately.** Do not wait until May. GACC processing times can run 4-8 weeks for first-time registrations. Contact the Nigeria Agricultural Quarantine Service (NAQS) or your export promotion authority (NEPC) to initiate the registration through official channels. You will need your facility's food safety certifications, production scope documentation, and a completed GACC registration form.

**Step 2 — Build farm-level traceability into your procurement.** Every batch you ship to China after June 2026 needs to be traceable to specific farms with GPS coordinates, farmer identification, and basic input records. This is the practical difference between a GACC registration that survives routine inspection and one that generates a customs query. If you are working with outgrowers or cooperatives, begin registering and mapping those farms now.

**Step 3 — Generate GACC-compliant documentation for each shipment.** Each shipment needs a phytosanitary certificate from NAQS, a certificate of origin, your GACC registration number on all shipping documents, and traceability records linking the shipment to its source batches and farms. A due diligence statement in the GACC format is increasingly expected for processed commodities.

1 June 2026 is not the end of GACC's compliance evolution.
